The following are my notes/quotation which I really liked:

"The beginning of pride is in heaven. The continuance of pride is on earth. The end of pride is in hell."

Definition of pride: a feeling or satisfaction derived from one's own achievements, the achievements of those with whom one is closely associated, or from qualities or possessions that are widely admired.

Definition itself says everything. I don't have many words to add.

Most humans, in varying degrees, are already dead. In one way or another they have lost their dreams, their ambitions, their desire for a better life. They have surrendered their fight for self-esteem and they have compromised their great potential. They have settled for a life of mediocrity, days of despair and nights of tears. They are no more than living deaths confined to cemeteries of their choice. Yet they need not remain in that state. They can be resurrected from their sorry condition. They can each perform the greatest miracle in the world. They can each come back from the dead... and in those books are the simple secrets, techniques, and methods which they can still apply to their own lives to become anything they wish to be and to attain all the true riches of life."

The author emphasizes book reading as a habit to reach success. In some sense, he is talking about books like Elliot's five foot collection. He encourages us to read books that will make you think, inculcate values. But he also says that he has already read most of the great books and found that they all teach the same thing albeit in different ways.¬†

He says that today people are forgetting their dreams ambitions etc. which have driven our life during our childhood. Instead we are living in a safe zone and have given up our ambitions. This is creating the whole problems. We are living like dead without any new hope etc.

most of us build prisons for ourselves and after we occupy them for a period of time we become accustomed to their walls and accept the false premise that we are incarcerated for life. As soon as that belief takes hold of us we abandon hope of ever doing more with our lives and of ever giving our dreams a chance to be fulfilled. We become puppets and begin to suffer living deaths. It may be praiseworthy and noble to sacrifice your life to a cause or a business or the happiness of others, but if you are miserable and unfulfilled in that lifestyle, and know it, then to remain in it is a hypocrisy, a lie, and a rejection of the faith placed in you by your creator.

He says, that we are blaming our situation/series of events/god for our bad luck. We donot understand that this was our fault. We forget that it was we whole have tried to create this wall in the name of future security. As time passes we get accustomed to the fate we have created and live like dead.

He says that instead of living a miserable life(even if you are financially successful), live a life which keeps us happy. We should live for a cause which will make our life a mark in history. Live for your ambition, childhood dream etc. We should start living for the purpose for which the creator has created us. Don't live in a lie, comparing your riches with a poor(may be he is more happy!), or comparing your literacy with an illiterate(may be he has more good moral values than yours.). It is better to live a life which will help uplift somebody's life for that can give you immense happiness which not even earning millions of dollars. Because we are humans and have evolved by living in a society. A smile in other person's face will make you more happy than million dollars in your bank account.

The way we perceive this world depends on how we see it. if you see yourself in a hell, then even heaven will look like hellüòÇ. We should be open and broad minded. We should avoid prejudice on every topic. Stop complaining.¬†

The best way to keep make yourself in heaven is to first de-clutter yourself. Do one thing at a time. This is my personal experience. For example, while reading a wikipedia article if I start opening all the hyperlinks, then at the end the overwhelming information makes me feel exhausted and at the end I will only have superficial knowledge about the main topic I wanted to read. Because our cognitive capacity is limited. Use it wisely.

Start to forgive
Start to believe god is with you
Start to beieve what ever is happening is for your good
Don't forget to take calculated risks......

Every person has his time. You should be patient. When time is ripe, when you are ready to serve your purpose, sometimes unknowingly you will be pushed by god to serve your purpose and make this world a beautiful place.

The way think, the way it will happen. So start being optimistic.

while we are all different, each unique in his or her own way, our lack of self-esteem which produced our failure is a universal sickness always originating from the complex of either anxiety, guilt, or inferiority... the three standard emotional problems recognized by most students of psychiatry.

Everybody has a unique purpose in this world. But we don't believe in that. We can do what ever can be done by any person on this planet. We can do what Jeff Bezos can do, we can do what Bill Gates can do.... We don't believe it because we are anxious(because of negative thoughts, fear of any harm, safety offered by safe zone. In sum, we are first of all feared of any new thing happening in our life.), guilt(because we continue to live in our past. We are not able to forgive ourselves. Of course regretting the past is good. But we should understand that once we have learnt the lesson, we should be steadfast not to repeat it. Everybody in life makes a mistake. There is no need to continue to live in past just because you have done/experienced something wrong. First thing you have to understand is to forgive others, forgive yourself. For this start with an apology keeping aside your pride!), inferiority(because you have failed in the past doesn't mean you shouldn't try again. First check the cause for your failure. Mostly the failure is because you have not believed yourself "completely" that you can do the task. This led to you only applying partial of yourself. The other reason is surrounding yourself with foolish and pessimistic people who don't believe in you or your ideology. Don't worry start afresh, believing completely yourself, surrounding yourself with "only few" optimistic people who believe in you and your ideology, have patience, perseverance. Dedicate yourself completely.)

And don't copy or imitate anyone. Do it your own way. If you solve your problem with some other's strategy it will not work and you will fail. You will be writing a different(difficult) answer for a easy question.

"One of the saddest things in life is that the only thing we can do for eight hours a day, day after day, is work. We can't eat for eight hours a day, nor drink for eight hours a day, nor make love for eight hours a day-all we can do for eight hours is work. In my opinion, the above quote says: experience is something which we can gain in many ways. There is no need to do a job just because you will gain experience. Instead strive for wisdom. If you want to gain experience(in the job that you love to do) for example on how to serve people, you can do it in many ways: start an NGO, become IAS, become Chief Minister, become Prime Minister.... Every path is different but you will learn a lot. Choose the path that you are comfortable with. And remember all the paths converge at the end depending upon your effort.
